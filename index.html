Pittsburgh Coffee Rankings

Finding the best brew in the 'Burgh—one espresso at a time.

After traveling the world and using various sites to find the best coffee shops, I wondered: why are we stuck with mediocre listicles for Pittsburgh? I'm rating local coffee shops by flavor, price, variety, seating, and hours. I will review local coffee chains, but don’t expect to see Starbucks or Dunkin here.

Info On Criteria

Price:

Times are a-changin’. The days of a $2 cup of coffee may be relics of the pre-pandemic, but that doesn’t mean that we shouldn’t be discriminating on how much our coffee costs. Our metrics are the espresso and the latte sans add-ons.

Variety of options:

Not all of us drink a black coffee. Some of us, myself included, like what is arguably more milkshake than coffee beverage at times. A great coffee shop caters to a spectrum of gastronomic experiences, and an exceptional one puts their own spin on our caffeine vices.

Seating Space:

There is (almost) nothing as disappointing as going to a coffee shop to read, do work, or just hang out and people watch than to leave empty-seated. For whatever reason, this is never discussed when discussing coffee shops. Third spaces need the chairs to put butts in seats.

Hours:

Pittsburgh can be a sleepy city. This is in direct opposition to the caffeination we crave. While I respect a shop that opens at 10am and closes at 2pm, there is a cost.
