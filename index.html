Pittsburgh Coffee Rankings: Beyond the Taste

Writing About

After traveling the world and using various sites to find the best coffee shops, I wondered: why are we stuck with mediocre listicles for Pittsburgh? I'm rating local coffee shops by price, variety, seating, and hours.
Note: I will review local coffee chains, but don't expect to see Starbucks or Dunkin here.

About My Rating System

Price:

Times are a-changin'. The days of a $2 cup of coffee may be relics of the pre-pandemic, but that doesn't mean that we shouldn't be discriminating on how much our coffee costs. Our metrics are the espresso and the latte (12 oz) sans add-ons.

Price Rating Scale (based on espresso + latte total cost):

  • ☕☕☕☕☕ = Under $6.00 total (excellent value)
  • ☕☕☕☕ = $6.00-$7.00 total (very good value)
  • ☕☕☕ = $7.00-$8.00 total (good value)
  • ☕☕ = $8.00-$9.00 total (fair value)
  • ☕ = Over $9.00 total (expensive)

Variety of options:

Not all of us drink a black coffee. Some of us, myself included, like what is at times arguably more milkshake than coffee beverage. A great coffee shop caters to a spectrum of gastronomic experiences, and an exceptional one puts their own spin on our caffeine vices.

Seating Space:

There is (almost) nothing as disappointing as going to a coffee shop to read, do work, or just hang out and people watch than to leave empty-seated. For whatever reason, this is never discussed when discussing coffee shops. These spaces need the chairs to put butts in seats.

Hours:

Pittsburgh can be a sleepy city. This is in direct opposition to the caffeination we crave. While I respect a shop that opens at 10am and closes at 2pm, there is a cost.

Hours Rating Scale (based on average daily operating hours):

  • ☕☕☕☕☕ = 13+ hours daily average (excellent availability)
  • ☕☕☕☕ = 10-12.9 hours daily average (very good availability)
  • ☕☕☕ = 8-9.9 hours daily average (good availability)
  • ☕☕ = 6-7.9 hours daily average (fair availability)
  • ☕ = Under 6 hours daily average (limited availability)

Why Not Flavor?

Long story short, tastes vary. I'm not nearly an expert on flavor, and there are already so many coffee sites that pretend to be; no need to crowd the space further.
